Research

My main interests are:

  • Empirical analysis of Git-based software repositories (see GitWorks on Github).
  • P2P algorithms for user preferences elicitation (see WUW on Github).
  • Design and the implementation of P2P evolutionary function optimization algorithms (see P2Poem on Sourceforge).
  • Distributed decentralized processing in a peer-to-peer fashion.
  • Epidemic protocols for overlay maintenance to support distributed algorithms in large-scale P2P networks.

Employment

I am currently a post-doc at INRIA - Rennes, France. I work on two research tasks:
-- Empirical analysis of opensource software repositories. The purpose is to understand nontrivial emergent patterns that characterize successful projects. The analysis is meant to deepen the comprehension of the dynamics that undergo software design and development processes, focusing on different scales.
-- Evolutionary algorithms for software diversification. I am collaborating at the design and the development of a simulation environment to test novel evolutionary techniques aimed at increasing software diversity, with the purpose of improving robustness and resilience of complex software architectures. This task is performed within the Diversify EU FET project.
